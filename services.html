What We Do

Our Services

Recovery is more than sobriety — it's rebuilding a life. We walk alongside our community every step of the way: from their first class to their first paycheck, first license, and first career.

🏋

Fitness-Based Recovery

Free group fitness classes open to anyone in recovery, building the physical and mental foundation for lasting sobriety.

📚

Life Skills Development

Practical one-on-one and group support for the real-world skills that make independence possible.

💼

Job Training & Placement

Credential-based career pathways in peer support and fitness, plus placement partnerships with employers across the trades and beyond.

Pillar 1

Fitness-Based
Recovery

Our group fitness classes aren't just workouts — they're a structured environment where individuals in recovery practice the same disciplines that make long-term sobriety possible.

🤽

Recovery Skills Through Movement

Every class is intentionally designed to develop the mental and behavioral skills recovery demands. When you show up at 5:30am, push through something hard, and finish alongside your community — you're practicing the same skills you'll need to stay sober.

Resilience Emotional Regulation Consistency Accountability Community Discipline
👥

Free & Open to All

Classes are completely free and open to anyone in the recovery community — whether you live in one of our houses, are referred by a partner organization, or simply walk through the door. No insurance. No fees. No barriers.

We hold multiple sessions per week at mornings and afternoons, plus a Saturday Friends & Family Day so that the people who love someone in recovery can join the movement too.

Pillar 2

Life Skills
Development

Getting sober is only the beginning. Many people in recovery face practical barriers that make independence feel out of reach. We meet people where they are and help them build the everyday skills they need to move forward.

📄

Resume Building

We help participants craft resumes that honestly and confidently represent who they are today — including gaps, growth, and the real work recovery takes.

🚗

Driver's License

We walk alongside participants through the process of obtaining or reinstating a driver's license — a fundamental step toward employment and independence.

💌

Email & Digital Basics

Setting up a professional email, navigating job boards, and using basic technology — skills that open doors to employment, housing, and communication.

💳

Financial Foundations

Opening a bank account, understanding pay stubs, and building basic budgeting habits to support a stable, sober life.

📋

Job Application Support

From filling out applications to preparing for interviews, we provide hands-on support at every step of the job search process.

🤝

Personal Navigation

Whether it's legal paperwork, benefits enrollment, or figuring out next steps — we help participants navigate systems that can feel overwhelming alone.

Pillar 3

Job Training
& Placement

One of our core QCO-qualifying services: we provide real career pathways — both through certifications our participants earn within our organization and through placement partnerships with employers in the trades and beyond.

Internal Pathways

Careers Within
Our Organization

For many participants, their first job in recovery is one they earn right here. We provide the training, mentorship, and hands-on experience to launch careers in peer support and group fitness.

Peer Support Specialist Participants who are living their recovery become credentialed peer support specialists — turning their lived experience into a career helping others navigate the same path.
Group Fitness Trainer We train and certify participants as group fitness instructors, giving them the credentials and confidence to lead classes, build community, and earn a living doing what saved their own life.
External Placement

Placement With
Partner Employers

We maintain active relationships with employers across multiple industries who are willing to hire people in recovery. We work with each participant to find the right fit — and stay involved through the transition.

Trades & Skilled Labor Construction, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, and other skilled trades — industries with strong demand and real opportunity for people ready to work hard.
Healthcare & Recovery Services Placement with partner treatment centers, behavioral health organizations, and community health providers.
Other Partner Organizations We continuously expand our employer network to open doors in retail, hospitality, logistics, and more — wherever our participants want to go.
Arizona QCO Tax Credit

Your Donation Funds
These Pathways

As a Qualifying Charitable Organization (QCO) recognized by the State of Arizona, donations to Barbell Saves Project qualify for a dollar-for-dollar state income tax credit — up to $470 for individuals and $938 for married couples filing jointly. Your contribution directly funds job training, life skills programming, peer support certifications, and fitness instruction — the services that create lasting change.
